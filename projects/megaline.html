Megaline — Descriptive & Inferential Statistics

Statistical analysis of telecom customer behavior to identify differences between mobile plan revenues.

Project Overview

This project focuses on applying descriptive and inferential statistics to explore patterns and distributions in customer behavior. The goal was to practice Python for real-world data analysis tasks.

Problem: Megaline, a telecom operator, offers two prepaid plans: Surf and Ultimate. The commercial department wants to understand which plan generates more revenue to optimize their advertising budget.

Technologies & Tools:
Python (pandas, numpy, scipy, matplotlib, seaborn, math)
Jupyter Notebook
VS Code

Exploratory Data Analysis

  • Average call duration by plan and month
  • Monthly minutes per plan
  • Average messages per plan
  • MB consumed per plan
  • Average monthly revenue per plan

Conclusion

  • Although no significant differences were found in average call duration, number of messages, or monthly data consumption between plans, a t-test showed a significant difference in mean revenue.
  • On average, the Ultimate plan generates more revenue, but Surf shows higher volatility due to some users generating extra charges.
  • It is recommended to review the minute allowance for Ultimate, as most users consume less than the offered amount. Users with atypical behavior might generate extra revenue if limits were adjusted.
  • Surf’s increasing data consumption pattern in the last months of the year suggests an opportunity for adjusting commercial strategy.
  • Overall, Megaline should focus its marketing strategy on atypical high-consumption users who generate the most revenue, either within existing plans or through a redesigned offer.
